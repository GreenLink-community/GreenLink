Our mission

Parks are where community happens. We keep them healthy.

GreenLink connects volunteers with the parks, trails, and waterways that need them most — turning a few hours on a Saturday into cleaner water, stronger green spaces, and closer neighbors.

What we believe

Three things we believe

01

Parks belong to everyone.

A healthy park should be free, safe, and open to every neighbor — regardless of what neighborhood it's in.

02

Small actions add up.

A single bag of trash won't fix a watershed. Fourteen cleanups and a thousand bags will. We show up, again and again.

03

Stewardship is learned together.

Every cleanup is a chance to teach — especially the kids who show up — that caring for a place is a habit, not an event.

Our work, in practice

Four ways we show up

Cleanups

Park & trail cleanups

Monthly volunteer cleanups at local parks, trails, and green spaces — trash and recycling sorted, logged, and hauled out responsibly.

Waterways

Clean water projects

Targeted cleanups along creeks, ponds, and shorelines, plus storm drain marking to keep pollutants out of local waterways.

Restoration

Planting & habitat restoration

Native planting days and invasive species removal to help parks recover long after the trash is gone.

Education

Community education

Short, hands-on sessions at every event so volunteers of all ages understand the "why" behind the work.
