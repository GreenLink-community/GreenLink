The story so far

From one park to a neighborhood habit

GreenLink was born on a Saturday morning when three neighbors noticed the same problem: the park they walked through every day was full of litter, and nobody seemed to be coming to clean it. They posted a few flyers, borrowed some gloves, and spent two hours filling trash bags. By the end, a dozen curious passersby had joined in.

That first cleanup became a second, then a monthly tradition. The idea was simple — show up, pick up, and invite anyone who wanted to help. Today, GreenLink is a volunteer-run network of neighbors, students, and local groups committed to keeping parks and waterways healthy.
